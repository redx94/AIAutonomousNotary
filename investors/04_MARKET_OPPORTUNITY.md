## Breaking Down the Market (Plain English)

Financial analysts use three terms to measure market potential. Here's what they mean and what our numbers look like:

---

### TAM — Total Addressable Market
**"If we captured 100% of the relevant market, how big is it?"**

**Our TAM: $12.6 Billion by 2033**

The global digital notarization market — including e-notary, remote online notarization (RON), and blockchain-based notarization — is currently $2.8 billion and growing at 17.5% annually. By 2033 it reaches $12.6 billion.

*Source: 2024 Digital Notary Market Report*

This growth is being driven by:
- Government mandates moving paperwork online (e.g., IRS, courts, land registries)
- Remote work culture creating demand for digital signing and certification
- International businesses that can't ship physical documents back and forth
- Insurance, real estate, and banking industries digitizing their document workflows

---

### SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market
**"Of the total market, how much can we realistically serve with our technology?"**

**Our SAM: $3 Trillion (Tokenized Real-World Asset Debt)**

Here's where it gets exciting. Beyond basic notarization, we are building the infrastructure that the entire Real-World Asset (RWA) tokenization industry needs. When financial institutions tokenize a real estate portfolio or bond issuance on the blockchain, every document involved — the deed, the contract, the legal filings — needs cryptographic certification. That's our service.

The U.S. SEC has projected that tokenized debt securities and real estate could reach **$1–$3 Trillion by 2030–2035.** Every dollar of that market generates document notarization and certification needs.

### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market
**"Realistically, what can we capture in 5 years?"**

**Our SOM: $250 Million in Annual Revenue**

By capturing just **2%** of the global notarization market and a tiny slice of RWA trading volume (0.01%), our models project **$250 million in recurring annual revenue potential** by Year 5. Our actual Year 5 target in our conservative model is $88.5M ARR — well within reach of $250M theoretical potential.
