# 📈 Financial Projections — 5-Year Forecast Explained

> **Where do the numbers come from, and can you trust them?**

---

## How to Read This Document

Financial projections for early-stage companies are estimates based on assumptions — not guarantees. What makes a projection credible is whether the assumptions behind it are realistic and clearly explained. We've done that here.

Every number in our model is tied to a specific, measurable marketplace behavior. We'll show you the logic behind each one.

## The Assumptions Behind Each Number

### Year 1: Beta Launch ($200K ARR)

**50,000 documents** — Is this realistic?
- DocuSign processes over 1 million documents *per day*
- The US alone has 150,000+ licensed notaries
- If we sign 5 enterprise clients (law firms averaging 200 docs/month), that's 12,000 documents from enterprise alone in Year 1
- 50,000 documents from a mix of enterprise pilots, direct users, and marketing = **very achievable**

**100 investment vaults** — Is this realistic?
- This is fewer vaults than the number of commercial real estate deals closed in a single mid-sized U.S. city in one month
- We only need early-adopter property owners willing to try a new, cheaper, faster alternative
- **Extremely conservative target**

---

### Year 2-3: Growth Phase ($1.35M → $7.85M ARR)

**250,000 → 1.2M documents/year**
- This is a 5x and 24x increase from Year 1
- This growth is fueled by: the Seed round funding (B2B sales, marketing), increasing RON legislation, and the network effect of the marketplace
- Compare with DocuSign: they grew from 100K to 1M users in roughly 24 months after their Series B

**$850M in marketplace trading volume by Year 3**
- Compare: Uniswap (a decentralized trading platform) reached $1B in daily trading volume within 18 months of launch
- Our marketplace is more specialized (legal documents, not crypto), so we expect slower but steadier growth
- $850M annually = ~$70M/month, which is a small fraction of the existing real estate transaction market

---

### Year 4-5: Scale Phase ($28M → $88.5M ARR)

**12M documents/year by Year 5**
- The US notarization market processes an estimated 1.25 billion notarizations per year
- 12M documents = less than **1% of the US market alone**
- If we have any international traction by Year 5, even 0.5% of global volume exceeds this target

**$10.5B in marketplace trading volume**
- The existing commercial real estate secondary market is $300B+/year in the US
- The tokenized real estate market alone (our core asset class) is projected at $1.4T globally by 2030
- $10.5B = less than 1% of that addressable market

## Why Our Model Is Conservative

The numbers above are intentionally set **well below** what comparable platforms have achieved:

| Comparable | What They Do | Revenue at 5 Years |
|---|---|---|
| **DocuSign** | Digital signatures (subset of our features) | $1.4 Billion ARR |
| **OpenSea** | NFT marketplace (similar mechanism) | $700M revenue at peak |
| **Coinbase** | Crypto exchange (similar fee model) | $7.7 Billion ARR |
| **Our Model** | Full stack: AI notarization + NFT + marketplace | $88.5M ARR target |

Our Year 5 target ($88.5M) is a fraction of what comparable companies achieved — and we have more features and a larger addressable market than any of them individually.

## The Valuation Math (How $30M Now Becomes Much More)

Here's the simple version of how early-stage valuations work:

**Step 1:** Our Year 3 forecast shows $7.85M in annual revenue.

**Step 2:** Fast-growing Web3 infrastructure companies (like Chainlink, Ondo Finance) are valued at **200x – 500x their annual revenue** because investors price in future growth.

**Step 3:** Even using just **20x revenue** (far below comparable industry multiples), Year 3 revenue of $7.85M implies a **$157 million network value** at Year 3.

**Step 4:** Apply a 40% annual discount rate (standard for early-stage venture risk) back to today — that justifies a current raise at **$25M – $35M pre-money valuation**.

**What this means for you:**
> Investing at a $30M valuation, with a conservative path to $157M+ in 3 years, represents a potential **5x return** on a conservative timeline — with upside scenarios (faster adoption, government contracts, international expansion) that could multiply that significantly.
