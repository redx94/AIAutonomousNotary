# 💰 Business Model — How We Make Money

> **Three distinct revenue streams. All automated. All growing.**

---

## Overview

AI Autonomous Notary generates revenue from three sources. What makes our model powerful is that all three are **algorithmic and automatic** — meaning once the infrastructure is running, revenue scales without needing to hire proportional staff. This is what investors call a "high-margin, scalable business."

---

## Revenue Stream 1: Document Notarization Fees
### (The Core Business)

**What It Is:**
Every time someone notarizes a document on our platform, we charge a flat fee.

**The Numbers:**
| | Traditional Notary | AI Autonomous Notary |
|---|---|---|
| Cost to customer | $15 – $300+ | **~$8 per document** |
| Our net revenue per document | N/A | **$3 (after operating costs)** |
| Time to complete | Days to weeks | Under 5 minutes |

**How It Breaks Down:**
Of each $8 fee:
- $3 goes to the AI validation network (the decentralized computers verifying the document)
- $2 covers infrastructure (blockchain gas fees, secure storage)
- **$3 is net revenue to the protocol (treasury / token buybacks)**

**Why This Is a Good Business:**
- Notarization is legally mandated — demand never disappears regardless of economic conditions
- We're 70% cheaper than traditional services, yet still profitable
- Volume potential is enormous: millions of documents per year, globally

## Revenue Stream 2: Marketplace Trading Fees
### (The High-Growth Engine)

**What It Is:**
Every time a fractional document share is bought or sold on our marketplace, we automatically capture a 0.5% fee. This is similar to how stock exchanges or crypto trading platforms make money on every trade.

**The Numbers (A Concrete Example):**
> A $1,000,000 commercial real estate deed is fractionalized.
> Investors trade $5,000,000 worth of those shares per year on our marketplace.
> **Protocol fee: 0.5% × $5,000,000 = $25,000 in passive annual revenue from one asset alone.**

Scale this across thousands of assets and the math becomes extraordinary:
- 1,000 assets averaging $5M/year in trading volume = **$25,000,000/year**
- 10,000 assets = **$250,000,000/year**

**Why This Scales So Well:**
This is the "network effect" — the more assets listed on our marketplace, the more investors it attracts, which makes the platform more attractive for listing more assets, which attracts more investors. It's a self-reinforcing growth loop.

## Revenue Stream 3: Document Lending & Escrow Fees
### (The Emerging Revenue Layer)

**What It Is:**
Once a document is notarized and its value is established on-chain, it can be used as collateral to borrow money — like using your home's equity to get a loan. Our platform charges a 1% origination fee on all such loans.

**The Opportunity:**
- $500,000 property deed used as collateral for a $250,000 business loan = **$2,500 in origination fees**
- As the document securities market matures, lending against notarized assets becomes a mainstream financial product
- This mirrors existing billion-dollar markets like mortgage origination and asset-backed lending

## What Happens to the Revenue?

The protocol distributes revenue in a way that benefits everyone in the ecosystem:

```
Every $1 of Protocol Revenue is split:

  40¢  →  Used to buy $NOTARY tokens from the open market and permanently destroy them
           (This reduces the token supply, which increases value for all holders)

  60¢  →  Distributed as real cash yield (in USDC or ETH) to $NOTARY token stakers
           (If you hold and stake tokens, you receive regular income payments)
```

This means **investors who hold $NOTARY tokens benefit from the protocol's growth in two ways:**
1. **Appreciation:** As more tokens are destroyed, the remaining ones become scarcer (and more valuable)
2. **Income:** As revenue grows, stakers receive larger regular yield payments

## Enterprise Revenue (B2B)

In addition to the consumer model above, major revenue potential comes from **enterprise contracts** with:

- **Real estate brokerages** integrating notarization into their closing workflow

- **Government agencies** digitizing public record notarization

Enterprise clients pay subscription fees for API access (think Stripe for legal documents), providing predictable, recurring contract revenue on top of the usage-based fees above.
