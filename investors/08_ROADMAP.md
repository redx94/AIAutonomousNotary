## Phase 2: The Marketplace (Months 7–18)
### 💰 This is what your investment funds

**Goal:** Build the trading infrastructure that turns our notarization service into a financial platform.

**What Gets Built:**

**The Trading Platform**
- A decentralized order book where investors can buy and sell fractional document shares
- An automated market maker (AMM) — think of it as a 24/7 automatic trading system that ensures there's always a buyer and seller available
- Auction capabilities for unique, high-value documents

**The DeFi Layer** *(Decentralized Finance)*
- A lending protocol where property owners can borrow against their notarized assets
- Insurance mechanisms to protect against systemic risks
- Staking pools where $NOTARY holders earn real income

**The $NOTARY Token Launch**
- ERC-20 token deployment
- Governance system goes live — token holders begin voting
- Initial liquidity on Uniswap (the world's largest decentralized exchange)

**Expected Outcome:**
- First external revenue begins flowing
- Institutional B2B enterprise pilots launch
- Platform reaches revenue-positive milestone

## Phase 3: Enterprise & Advanced AI (Months 19–30)
### 🤖 Making the AI smarter and opening the enterprise market

**Goal:** Upgrade the AI systems and build enterprise-grade features to capture law firms, banks, and real estate companies.

**What Gets Built:**

**Advanced AI Capabilities**
- Natural language processing — the AI reads and understands document language, not just detects tampering
- Computer vision — analyzes physical signature characteristics and handwriting
- Fraud detection models trained on real document fraud databases
- Dynamic risk scoring for each document asset type

**Privacy & Security Upgrades**
- Zero-knowledge proof system — allows verification without exposing document contents
- Quantum-resistant cryptography — protects against future computing threats (this is forward-thinking infrastructure that major banks are now requiring)

**Multi-Chain Expansion**
- Bridge to Polygon network for lower-cost transactions (making the platform accessible to smaller investors and frequent users)
- Multi-chain compatibility — major document assets accessible across blockchain networks

## Phase 4: Regulatory Compliance (Months 31–36)
### ⚖️ Becoming fully legal in every major jurisdiction

**Goal:** Achieve regulatory certifications that make us the trusted, legally-approved platform for institutional clients.

**What Gets Built:**

**U.S. Regulatory Alignment**
- SEC-compliant security token framework (required to offer fractional document investments to the public)
- Alternative Trading System (ATS) registration — this is the legal license required to operate a securities marketplace
- Regulatory reporting automation — the platform automatically files required reports with regulators

**Privacy Compliance**
- GDPR compliance for European markets
- HIPAA compliance for medical document categories
- Right-to-erasure mechanisms (the ability to remove personal data while keeping document records intact)

**KYC/AML Integration**
- Decentralized identity verification that works across jurisdictions
- Anti-money laundering monitoring built into the protocol

---

## Phase 5: Global Scale (Months 37–48)
### 🌍 Going enterprise and going global

**Goal:** Enterprise partnerships, international expansion, and full DAO governance.

**What Gets Built:**

**Enterprise API Platform**
- Full API suite for law firms, banks, and real estate companies to integrate our notarization directly into their existing software
- White-label option — large companies can operate private versions of the platform under their own brand
- Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for institutional clients

**Full Decentralized Governance**
- DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) fully operational
- Token holders govern all major decisions
- Protocol treasury managed by community vote

**International Markets**
- Multi-jurisdictional support — notarization is legally valid across different countries
- International recognition frameworks for cross-border documents
- Localized versions for non-English markets
