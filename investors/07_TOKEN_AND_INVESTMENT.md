## Part 1: The $NOTARY Token

### What Is a Token?

In traditional companies, investors receive **equity (shares of stock)**. In a decentralized protocol, investors can receive **tokens** — which serve a similar purpose but with additional built-in utility.

The $NOTARY token is the native currency and governance mechanism of the AI Autonomous Notary protocol. Think of it as a combination of:
- **Equity:** Token holders benefit when the platform grows (through scarcity and revenue sharing)
- **Utility:** Tokens have specific uses on the platform (fee discounts, voting rights, marketplace access)
- **Yield Instrument:** Token stakers receive a share of platform revenue, similar to a dividend

### What Does "Vesting" Mean?

Vesting means your tokens are released to you gradually over time, rather than all at once. It protects all parties:
- It protects other investors by preventing any single party from flooding the market with tokens immediately
- It aligns incentives — token holders benefit from staying engaged with the platform

**Your vesting schedule (Strategic Investor):**
- Months 0–6: Lock-up period (tokens allocated but not transferable)
- Months 7–18: Tokens release linearly (approximately each month you receive your proportional share)
- After 18 months: All tokens fully vested and transferable

## Part 2: How the $NOTARY Token Gains Value

### Mechanism 1: Revenue-Driven Buybacks (Scarcity)

40% of all protocol revenue is automatically used to **purchase $NOTARY tokens on the open market and permanently destroy them**. This is called a "buy-and-burn" mechanism.

**Why This Matters:**
- There are 100 million tokens — fixed forever
- Every month, some are destroyed (removed from circulation)
- Basic economics: as supply decreases and demand stays the same or grows, price increases
- By Year 3, at $7.85M in annual revenue, approximately **$3.1M worth of tokens** would be burned annually

This is analogous to a company using profits for stock buybacks — a mechanism that has historically created significant shareholder value.

---

### Mechanism 2: Revenue Yield (Income)

60% of all protocol revenue is distributed **as real cash income** (paid in USDC or ETH) to token holders who "stake" their tokens.

**Staking** means voluntarily locking your tokens for a period of time to support the network, in return for income payments.

**Projected Staking Yield (Year 1):**
If 30% of circulating supply is staked by Year 1 (a conservative assumption):
- 6 million tokens in staking rewards distributed
- Estimated **20% APR** for staking participants
- Plus variable fee revenue share on top

---

### Mechanism 3: Platform Utility (Demand)

The more widely the platform is used, the more $NOTARY tokens are needed:

- **Law firms** must hold/stake $NOTARY to access the enterprise API tier
- **Oracle operators** (the AI nodes that verify documents) must stake **50,000 $NOTARY** each as security deposit — this permanently locks tokens off the market
- **Investors** use $NOTARY to get priority access and fee discounts in the marketplace
- **Governance voters** need $NOTARY to vote on platform decisions

Every new enterprise client, every new oracle node, every new marketplace participant creates **new demand for a fixed, shrinking supply.**

### What Is a SAFT?

A **SAFT** (Simple Agreement for Future Tokens) is the blockchain industry equivalent of a convertible note. It is a legally recognized investment instrument used by most major crypto projects. You invest now, and receive tokens when they become available at mainnet launch. SAFTs have been used to raise billions from institutional investors including Andreessen Horowitz, Sequoia, and others.

## Part 4: Governance — Your Voice in the Protocol

As a $NOTARY holder, you have the right to vote on key decisions:
- Changing platform fee rates
- Approving treasury spending proposals
- Voting on protocol upgrades
- Adding new jurisdictions or asset classes

Every $NOTARY = 1 vote. All votes are recorded on-chain (publicly and permanently). No single entity can overrule a majority vote — including the founding team. This is called **decentralized governance**, and it's the gold standard for fair, trustless protocol management.
